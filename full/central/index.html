Chapter 01 · Region

Central Switzerland

Waterfalls, glacier lakes and iron bridges strung across gorges so deep they hum. The heart of this guide. Twelve spots from Adelboden to the Grimsel Pass.

WATERFALLS

Engstligen Falls

Two-tier falls off the Engstligenalp plateau above Adelboden. Overcast weather is actually the flex here.

Place your subject in front of the bright white parts of the waterfall to make them pop. Zoom in for cleaner compositions. Sometimes just water behind the subject is the strongest frame.

If you have a drone, send it up. Aerial angles give you perspectives you cannot get from the ground. Just watch the spray if you fly close.

Waterfalls make great portrait spots too. Wet clothes, flying droplets, mist in the air, all of it reads as cinematic.

RegionAdelboden, Central Switzerland
AccessCable car + short walk
EffortEasy
Best lightWhen cloudy

WATERFALLS

Oltschibachfall

One of the tallest single-drop falls in the canton. Best admired from the viewpoint trail, not the base.

Great pictures from far and near. But I would not recommend hiking all the way to the base. There is no clear path, the rocks are slippery, and a fall here could be fatal. Not worth it.

Better to admire it from a safe distance and still get great shots. Late afternoon light on the cliff face is when the colours really come alive.

RegionBrienz, Central Switzerland
AccessHike from Brienzersee
EffortModerate
Best lightLate afternoon

VALLEYS

Rosenlaui

Tucked into the Reichenbach valley, where the Wetterhorn feels unreal in the late afternoon light.

No need to go all the way up to Rosenlaui village. The plateau five minutes before makes the best image of the mountain in the back. The Wetterhorn. Framed by pines and meadow.

Park in Meiringen, take the postbus, then walk the flat trail for twenty minutes until the valley opens up.

RegionMeiringen, Central Switzerland
AccessPostbus + walk
EffortEasy
Best lightAfternoon

WATERFALLS

Rosenlaui Public Waterfall

A gorge carved into the mountain next to the hotel. Paid entry, but the light is worth it.

It costs a few francs to enter but it is very nice. Definitely bring a tripod to be able to properly expose the shot. The gorge is dark, the water is lit from above, and handheld will not cut it.

First light in the morning is the sweet spot. A slow shutter turns the spray into silk; a fast one catches every droplet.

RegionRosenlaui, Central Switzerland
AccessWalk from Rosenlaui hotel
EffortEasy
Best lightMorning

WATERFALLS

Rosenlaui Secret Waterfall

The one most visitors never find. Hidden behind the hotel, almost empty most mornings.

At the restaurant next to the parking lot, you can walk back to this waterfall. Very few people know. My recommendation: hit the public falls first, eat the ravioli at the restaurant, then hike back to the secret one.

Wear a colourful jacket. Otherwise you will disappear into the dark rock in the photo. Even a small splash of red or orange changes the composition completely.

RegionRosenlaui, Central Switzerland
AccessWalk behind the hotel
EffortEasy
Best lightAny time

SUSPENSION BRIDGES

Triftbrücke

One of the most dramatic suspension bridges in the Alps. 170 m long, strung across a glacier lake.

Park in Gadmen and take the Triftbahn cable car. From the top station, it is a steep uphill hike to the bridge. About 410 metres of elevation, around two hours of walking.

Bring a drone. The aerial angles here are the real payoff. Bridge, lake, glacier, all in one frame. After sunset gives you the softest light on the glacier face.

RegionGadmen, Central Switzerland
AccessCable car + 2 hour hike
EffortModerate. Steep
Best lightAfter sunset

GLACIER LAKES

Triftsee

The turquoise lake behind the bridge. Same hike, different light, the place where I would actually spend the night.

I recommend camping up there if you want to catch both sunset and sunrise. The cable car does not run early enough or late enough, so camping is the only way to get those shots.

Set up above the treeline, stay discreet, leave no trace. The lake goes from glass-still in the morning to glacier-fed current by afternoon.

RegionGadmen, Central Switzerland
AccessCable car + 2 hour hike
EffortModerate
Best lightSunset or sunrise

GLACIER LAKES

Gelmersee

Easy to reach, and the funicular that gets you there is one of the steepest in the world. A photo spot in itself.

Drive to the base of the Gelmerbahn, where you will take one of the steepest funiculars in the world up to the lake. It only runs in summer and gets busy. Check if it is open, and book your seat in advance.

Morning light paints the far cliffs gold and turns the water glassy. Worth the early start.

RegionHaslital, Central Switzerland
AccessFunicular (summer only)
EffortEasy
Best lightMorning

CABLE CARS

Gelmerbahn

The ride up. Cinematic enough to go viral on its own. 106% gradient at the steepest section.

Take a timelapse in the front or back row of the cable car for a viral video. The drop opens up behind you and the whole valley unfolds in thirty seconds.

Booking the front seat is worth the extra effort. Keep your camera steady. The wobble is part of the charm but easy to over-do.

RegionHaslital, Central Switzerland
AccessFunicular from base
EffortEasy
Best lightAny time

CAMPER SPOTS

Guttannen Valley

Quiet, scenic, easy to park in for the night. A sleeper base camp for the Grimsel and Gelmer runs.

The valley between Innertkirchen and the Grimsel Pass is quiet and scenic, with plenty of spots to park and sleep in a camper van. Low traffic, good cell signal along the main road.

If you are visiting Gelmersee, this valley makes a great place to spend the night nearby. Thirty minutes to the funicular in the morning.

GORGES

Aare Gorge

Aareschlucht. One of the most impressive canyons in Switzerland. A narrow bridge runs right along the gorge wall.

There is an entrance fee, so check the opening hours before you go. It can get crowded, which makes it tricky to take clean shots without people in them.

You will be walking on a narrow wooden walkway built along the canyon wall. Slow shutter on the water, fast on the light shafts. And be patient for a gap in the foot traffic.

RegionMeiringen, Central Switzerland
AccessPaid entry
EffortEasy
Best lightWhen cloudy
Open in Maps Photo · @leon.helg

SUMMITS

Brienzer Rothorn

The peak above Brienz. Cable car to the top, and a camp lake below the summit that almost nobody photographs right.

Two ways up. The Brienz Rothorn Bahn is a steam cog railway from Brienzersee, slow and scenic. The Sörenberg cable car is the fast side. Both drop you within a short walk of the summit.

The shot is not from the top. Walk down toward the tarns below the south face in the evening. On a still day the whole ridge reflects in the water. Bring a tent, the light at first wind is the quiet window.

This is also the starting point for the full Hardergrat traverse. If you are camping here anyway, you are already halfway to that hike.

RegionBrienz, Central Switzerland
AccessCog railway or cable car
EffortEasy to summit
Best lightEvening, still wind
Open in Maps Photo · @oliwear.j

RIDGES

Hardergrat Trail

The big one. Twelve hours along a knife-edge ridge from Brienzer Rothorn to Harder Kulm, no water the whole way.

Take the cable car up to the Brienzer Rothorn (either from Brienz or from Sörenberg) and start the hike there. It ends at Harder Kulm above Interlaken.

The whole hike takes about twelve hours and has no water sources. Carry everything you need. Not an easy hike. Take it seriously.

RegionBrienz, Central Switzerland
AccessCable car + full day hike
EffortHard. No water
Best lightAll day
Open in Maps Photo · @leon.helg

RIDGES

Tannhorn

The highlight of the Hardergrat. A perfect grass ridge that peaks at sunset.

Heading west from Brienzer Rothorn, the walk to Tannhorn takes about an hour and a half to two. It sits right in the middle of the Hardergrat, the point where the ridge narrows to a clean line of grass with drops on both sides.

This is the best frame on the whole traverse. Sunset paints the west face warm while the east stays cool in shadow, so the ridge reads as a blade against the sky. Figure in frame gives scale.

If you shoot it at sunset, you cannot make the last cable car back. Plan to bivy on the ridge or carry on through the dark to Harder Kulm. Either way, pack for cold.

RegionBrienz, Central Switzerland
AccessCable car to Rothorn + 2 hour hike
EffortModerate on the ridge
Best lightSunset
Open in Maps Photo · @leon.helg

LAKES

Lake Lucerne

Vierwaldstättersee. The lake that runs through half of Central Switzerland, with fingers that disappear between the cliffs.

You cannot really shoot "Lake Lucerne" from a single spot. The lake has four arms, and every town on it has a different frame. Lucerne old town for the postcard, Bürgenstock for the cliff edge, Morschach for the Fronalpstock view, Brunnen for the Rütli angle.

Winter is when this place actually reads as alpine. Fresh snow on the pine slopes, dark water in the lake, the Alps stacked behind. Summer is busy and the light is flatter.

If you only have time for one, go up Fronalpstock on a clear winter morning. You can see the full length of the lake and Mount Pilatus catches the first light.

RegionCentral Switzerland
AccessTrain, boat, or cable car
EffortEasy
Best lightWinter sunrise

VIEWPOINTS

Fronalpstock

Lake Lucerne laid out below, sunset turning the whole basin pink. Cable car up from Morschach, a short walk to the bench.

Take the cable car from Morschach Talstation up to Fronalpstock. Walk ten minutes along the ridge to the main viewpoint. Stone bench, zero work.

Aim for sunset. The sun drops right behind Mount Pilatus and the light catches the full length of the lake. In winter the snow cover pushes the contrast even further.

Wide first, then swap to a longer lens for the compressed version of Pilatus and the Vierwaldstättersee fingers.

RegionMorschach, Central Switzerland
AccessCable car + 10 min walk
EffortEasy
Best lightSunset

HIDDEN GEMS

Fulberg

A ridge hike above the Grimsel region that almost nobody does. Wide open, quiet, and the photo pays for the climb.

Park at the trailhead near Grimsel Hospiz and hike up. It is a steady climb with no technical sections, but the ridge exposes you to weather fast, so pick a clear window.

Plan to stay late or sleep up there. The light two hours before sunset is when the ridge reads best. Bring a shell, water, and layers even in summer.

If you want the frame you see here, walk past the first viewpoint and keep going. The first bench is the crowded one. The second is empty.

RegionGrimsel, Central Switzerland
AccessDrive + ridge hike
EffortModerate
Best lightLate afternoon or sunset
Open in Maps Photo · @leon.helg

WATERFALLS

Iffigfalle

Above Lenk, best in May and June when snowmelt pushes the falls into their fullest.

The waterfall is close to the road but the best angle requires a quick hike above the parking area. May and June is peak water time - if you come later, plan for smaller volume.

Overcast light works well here. The falls freeze in winter but are less dramatic without the snowmelt pressure.

RegionLenk, Central Switzerland
AccessDrive + short walk
EffortEasy
Best lightMay to June

RIDGES

Schrattenfluh

A dramatic ridge hike from Marbachegg cable car, overnight at Heftihütte.

Take the cable car up to Marbachegg, then hike via Imbrig to Heftihütte. The ridgeline from there gives you alpine views without the crowds of more famous peaks.

Bring a light tent if you want sunrise - the hut fills up fast. Drone shots of the ridge work well in golden hour.

RegionEmmental, Central Switzerland
AccessCable car + 3 hour hike
EffortModerate
Best lightSunrise
