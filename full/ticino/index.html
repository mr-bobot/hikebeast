A FREEZING DIP UNDER A WATERFALL

Cascata La Froda

A tall waterfall above Sonogno with a plunge pool cold enough to wake the dead. Jumping in is half the experience.

Drive to Sonogno and follow the short hike to the falls. The pool underneath is deep and ice-cold even in summer. Jumping in is basically tradition at this point.

Cloudy days are ideal. The trees stay green, the whites stay readable. On sunny days, mornings before direct light hits the falls are the workable window.

THE ARCH, THE JUMP, THE ICON

Ponte dei Salti

The double-arched stone bridge in Lavertezzo. Twelve metres up, cold river underneath. Send it if you're up for it.

Park in Lavertezzo and walk to the bridge. The jump is about 12 meters and the water is freezing even in August. Check the river level and for any rocks before you commit.

Anytime light is fine. The canyon shade keeps contrast manageable most of the day. Early morning is quietest. Late afternoon gets the most swimmers and best atmosphere.

THE WHOLE VALLEY IS THE SPOT

Valle Verzasca

Turquoise river, bleached-white boulders, all the way up the valley. Pick any pull-off and you'll find something.

The whole Valle Verzasca is worth driving end to end. Rock formations along the river create natural pools, small jumps, swimming holes. Keep your eye out as you drive.

CURRENT IS SERIOUS. People drown in this river every summer because they underestimate the flow after rain. Check conditions, don't jump into water you haven't scouted.

THE VILLAGE AT THE HEART OF IT

Lavertezzo

The stone village by the bridge. Narrow lanes, old slate roofs, and the river running through the whole scene.

Walk past the bridge into the village itself. Slate-roofed stone houses, tiny chapels, lanes that still feel medieval. Worth half a morning even if you came just for the water.

Light is soft most of the day thanks to the valley walls. Late afternoon when the sun drops behind the ridge is when the stone goes warm and the village lights switch on.

RIVERS

Ponte Brolla

A cliff jumping spot on the Brolla river - crystal water and pure adrenaline.

This is not a hiking destination, it is a cliff-jumping destination. Locals know it well. If you are not jumping, the river scenery still reads well in photos.

Come in summer when the water is warm and high enough. The rock formations and the turquoise water are the real draw.

WATERFALLS

Cascata di Foroglio

At the end of Valle Maggia, a waterfall dropping into the valley.

Drive as far as you can into Valle Maggia, then hike to the waterfall. The valley is tight and lush - the light cuts through in shafts.

May and June are best for volume. The mist and spray here are intense - bring a lens cloth.

IF YOU HAVE THE LEVEL. OR A FRIEND WHO DOES

Valle Verzasca Kayak

The Verzasca is famous for whitewater kayaking. Not something you just try. But if you have the skill, or a friend who does, the shots are unreal.

The valley holds serious whitewater. Only experienced kayakers should be on the water here; the rocks and currents don't forgive mistakes. If you're shooting and not paddling, team up with a kayaker who knows the lines.

A drone captures the scale. Turquoise water, bleached boulders, a tiny kayak tracking through the curves. Mid to late summer is when flow eases to navigable levels; spring runoff is expert-only.
