LAKES

Riffelsee

The Matterhorn, doubled. At first light the tip turns red before the rest of the mountain catches up.

Take the Gornergrat Bahn from Zermatt up to Rotenboden. From the station it is a short walk down to the lake. Maybe ten minutes on an easy path.

Shoot wide. 24mm or 35mm. To keep the peak and its reflection in the same frame. The reflection only works if the wind stays down, so cross your fingers for a still morning.

Be in position before sunrise. The red on the tip lasts maybe five minutes before full light floods in and kills the effect.

RegionZermatt, Valais
AccessGornergrat Bahn
EffortEasy
Best lightSunrise

VILLAGES

Zermatt Village

You do not need to leave town for the shot. The village itself is half the frame.

Rooftops and balconies work for the Matterhorn-over-chalets look. The view from Hotel Rex is one of the cleanest in town if you can get a room on the right side.

Night is underrated here. Lit windows and street lamps against the dark pyramid read as cinematic on their own. You do not need to do much.

A few minutes outside the village, you can line up the Matterhorn and the lit chalets in the same composition. That is the one that goes viral.

VIEWPOINTS

Riffelalp

One stop before everyone else gets off. The Matterhorn layered with larches. Fog if you are lucky.

Same Gornergrat Bahn as for Riffelsee, but get off earlier at Riffelalp. Ten to fifteen minutes on the trail brings you to a quieter viewpoint.

Cloudy mornings are the flex. Fog moving through the larches gives you layers you cannot get on a clear day. And fewer tourists in every frame.

Pack a small tele (70 to 200mm) if you have it. The compression stacks the ridge, the trees, and the peak in a way a wide angle cannot.

AccessGornergrat Bahn + walk
EffortEasy

GLACIERS

Gorner Glacier

Scale is the whole story here. You want the biggest frame you can get your hands on.

Best from above. Fly a drone where the rules let you, or book a scenic flight out of Raron if you want the real panoramic. From the ground, the Gornergrat platform is the easy option.

From Gornergrat, a telephoto compresses the ice in a way a wide angle just cannot match. 100 to 400mm isolates the texture of the crevasses.

Go on a clear day. Fresh snow hides everything; clear light lets the blue in the ice read.

ABANDONED HOUSES, SURREAL LIGHT

The Ghost Town

A cluster of empty wooden huts above Zermatt that looks lifted out of a film set. Especially eerie in winter.

Walk up from Zermatt village toward Furi station. The old Weiler (hamlet) sits just off the trail, still standing but long since emptied. Dark wood against snow, zero tourists, and a perfect frame of the Matterhorn poking up behind.

Go at late afternoon or on an overcast day. Hard sun blows out the wood texture; soft light makes the timber glow. Fifteen minutes on foot from town, so easy to combine with a coffee after.

SWITZERLAND'S LARGEST, FOR NOW

Aletsch Glacier

The biggest glacier in the Alps, a 23-kilometre river of ice you can walk beside. Visibly shrinking year over year.

Start from Bettmeralp or Fiescheralp. Cable cars bring you up fast. The classic viewpoint is Eggishorn (2,927m), reached by another lift, for the full sweep of the glacier curving south. On a clear day you can see the Matterhorn on the horizon.

Drone shots are where this spot comes alive. Check local drone rules (parts sit inside a UNESCO protected zone) and fly respectfully. If you would rather stay grounded, the Aletsch Panorama trail runs along the ridge for a few hours of uninterrupted views.

AccessCable car + lift
EffortEasy to moderate

SLEEP HIGH, EAT WELL

Cabane Becs de Bosson

A mountain hut above Grimentz with views in every direction. Book ahead. It fills up fast, especially around sunset.

Cable car up from Grimentz, then a 2-hour hike to the hut at around 2,985m. The approach is mostly on packed dirt trail; winter visits need avalanche awareness and proper gear.

Arrive in time for dinner, stay the night, and catch both sunset and sunrise from the ridge. The Weisshorn and Dent Blanche dominate the east; the Valais Alps roll out west. One of the easiest huts in the canton to get huge views from.

A HUT ABOVE THE ICE

Moiry Glacier

A small lake at the foot of the glacier, a hut perched above, and light that hits the ice face at sunset.

Drive or take the bus from Grimentz up to Lac de Moiry, then hike up the moraine trail. Steep but well-marked. The cabane at the top is a good lunch stop and a solid base for photos late in the day when the glacier goes pink.

Bring a wide lens for the hut-and-glacier composition and a tele for the ice detail. The glacier is retreating visibly. The hiking approach shifts a bit year to year, so follow current signage.

FROM THE AIR

Moiry from Above

A drone perspective turns the moraine into abstract geometry. Blue ice, grey rubble, and the hut as a tiny orange dot.

If you have got a drone and the rules allow it, climb above the hut before launching and you will get a top-down that separates the glacier crevasse field cleanly from the ice lake below. Mid-morning light works best. Enough shadow to read the texture, not so low you lose the detail.

Handheld alternative: the trail continues up toward the Sassenaire ridge and gives you almost the same angle without needing any airspace permissions. Takes another hour from the hut.

DEEP WINTER ONLY, AND ONLY IF IT'S COLD

The Ice Cave

A natural tunnel under Zinal Glacier. One of the most photogenic spots in Valais. And one of the easiest to get hurt in if you go at the wrong time.

Start near coords 46.127840, 7.629728 and hike toward 46.071559, 7.632709. This is a deep-winter spot. Never go after warm days, and do not go alone. Ice caves collapse when temperatures rise; people die from that every year in the Alps. Check avalanche reports, tell someone your plan.

Inside, light bounces blue off the ice. A wide lens gets the whole tunnel arc; a mid-telephoto picks out the frozen detail. Bring crampons or microspikes, headlamp, and a tripod. It is darker than it looks.

RegionZinal
AccessWinter hike, cold-dependent
EffortHard / technical
Best lightMidday in deep winter

THE SAME PLACE, 12 HOURS LATER

The Ice Cave by Night

Headlamp, tripod, and 30 seconds of patience. The tunnel becomes something else entirely.

Same location, same winter-only rules. But shot after dark with the light source inside the cave. Set the camera to f/2.8, ISO around 1000, 20 to 30 second exposure. Have a friend stand still at the far end holding a warm-temperature headlamp; the ice walls will glow.

Dress warmer than you think. You will be standing in a wind tunnel made of ice. Kit that survives: weather-sealed body, two batteries kept inside your jacket, hand warmers taped to the lens to stop fogging when you exit.

A GLACIER YOU CAN STILL REACH ON FOOT

Ferpècle Glacier

Tucked at the head of the Val d'Hérens, quieter than its neighbours and wild in a way Zermatt no longer is.

Drive to the hamlet of Ferpècle at the end of the road, park, and walk in toward the glacier front. About 2 hours of mostly flat to gently rising terrain. You will share the valley with a handful of day hikers and some very serious-looking goats.

Bring a drone if you have one. The glacier and the surrounding Dent Blanche massif are best read from a bit of altitude. On the ground, look for the ice caves that form in the terminal face in late winter.

AccessDrive + 2 hr walk

ABSTRACT FROM ABOVE

Ferpècle River

The meltwater braid downstream of the glacier. From a drone, it looks painted.

Same approach as the glacier. On the way in or out, get airborne above the flats where the river fans across the gravel bed. Grey silt, pale blue channels, and sharp dark lines where the water bites into the sediment.

Shoot top-down between 10am and 2pm for the cleanest separation between water and stone. The pattern changes through the day as the glacier melts faster in the afternoon, so you can revisit the same spot and get a different frame.

THE EASIEST MARMOTS IN SWITZERLAND

Spielboden

A station above Saas Fee where the alpine marmots come within arm's reach. If you have ever wanted the shot, this is where.

Cable car up from Saas Fee to Spielboden (2,452m). The marmots are genuinely habituated. They will pose, sniff your lens, and occasionally take a nap at your feet. Peanuts are fine in moderation; do not feed them anything else.

Go in morning or late afternoon for soft light. A 70 to 200mm is plenty; you will not need anything longer. If you are after the classic Allalinhorn backdrop, walk 10 minutes up from the station toward the Hannig trail intersection.

13,000 YEARS OF ICE, RETREATING FAST

Saas Fee Glacier

A ski glacier that doubles as a summer photo location. Especially from the rotating restaurant at 3,500m.

Mittelallalin is the reachable high point: cable car from Saas Fee to Felskinn, then the Metro Alpin funicular inside the mountain. The terrace gives you a panorama of the ice fields and the Mischabel peaks. Crowded in ski season, empty in the shoulder months.

If you want to work the glacier edge on foot, the traverse from Spielboden to Längfluh is the best option. A 3-hour route that gets you close to the ice without needing crampons or a guide.

DROWNED TREES, STILL STANDING

The Flooded Forest

A rockslide in 1714 dammed a valley and left the forest underwater. The tree skeletons are still there, frozen mid-sentence.

Derborence Lake, east end. Drive up from Aven or hike in from the hamlet of Derborence itself. The flooded trees are visible in low water. Best chances are late summer into early autumn when the lake level drops.

Overcast light is your friend here; harsh sun flattens the reflection. Wide lens for the whole scene; a tele picks out individual trunks. This is also the most protected nature reserve in Valais. Stay on marked paths.

A VILLAGE AT THE MOUTH OF A GLACIER

La Fouly

A quiet hamlet at the head of Val Ferret, facing a wall of peaks that look borrowed from Patagonia.

Drive up the Val Ferret from Orsières. La Fouly sits under the Dolent, Tour Noir, and Grandes Jorasses. You are staring straight at the Swiss-Italian-French border. In winter it is a tiny ski resort; in summer the trails to Mont Dolent are some of the quietest in the Alps.

For photos, walk 15 minutes out of the village toward the glacier moraine. The alpenglow hits the peaks about 20 minutes before sunset. You will want a tele (135 to 200mm) to compress the layers.

A PASS WITH A LAKE ON TOP

Sanetschpass

A high mountain pass on the border of Valais and Bern, with a dam, a lake, and a cable car that drops into nothing.

Drive up from Sion through Conthey to the pass at 2,252m. The road is paved but narrow; check it is open (typically late May through October). At the top: the Sanetschsee reservoir, a restaurant, and a very dramatic cable car that descends vertically to Gsteig on the Bern side.

For photos, walk 30 minutes west along the dam toward the open meadows. The ridges catch morning light cleanly. A drone works well up here. No crowds, no airspace restrictions to worry about.

A CANYON HUNG WITH IRON WALKWAYS

Gorges du Durnand

A narrow gorge outside Martigny with bolted walkways clinging to the wall. Walk through it and the water is three metres beneath your boots.

Drive to Bovernier and follow signs to the gorges entrance (small entry fee). The loop takes about 45 minutes; wear grippy shoes because the wood gets slick from spray. The walkways zigzag up the falls, giving you angles you cannot get any other way.

Camera-wise: a wide lens and a variable ND for long exposures on the water. The lighting is mixed. Pockets of shade and shafts of direct sun through the canyon. So bracket exposures or shoot RAW and pull highlights later.

NARROWER, OLDER, LESS KNOWN

Gorges du Dailley

Durnand's smaller cousin near Salvan. Same walkway trick, half the tourists, and arguably more atmospheric.

Park near the village of Salvan and walk in. The trail is signposted. The wooden bridges are older and more weathered than Durnand's, which photographs beautifully. Waterfalls stack one above the next for almost a kilometre.

Best shot mid-morning when the light filters down through the canopy. In heavy rain seasons the spray gets intense, so protect the camera. The return route loops back through larch forest that turns gold in October.

A SHORT VERTICAL WITH BIG CONSEQUENCE

Nax Via Ferrata

A bolted climbing route above Nax village. Not long, not super technical. But genuinely exposed.

Park at 46.200456, 7.031477 and walk to the route start. You need a full via ferrata kit (harness, Y-lanyard with energy absorber, helmet, gloves) and some climbing experience. It is rated around K3. Not a beginner's route, but doable for a confident scrambler.

The view from the top traverses the Rhone valley. For photos, a drone captures the route and the climbers against the drop; from the ground, shoot from below with a wide lens to emphasize the exposure. Go early. Afternoon thunderstorms are common in summer.

THE SKI STATION IN SUMMER

Crans-Montana

Off-season, the empty slopes look like someone drained a golf course and left it. In the best possible way.

From Crans-Montana village, take any of the lifts up (most run summer service). The interesting photos are on the upper slopes above 2,000m. Wide-open meadows, scattered chalets, and long sightlines toward the Bernese Alps across the valley.

I come here for moody morning fog that rolls up the slope and disappears by 10am. The Plaine Morte glacier is just above. Worth a full-day visit if the weather holds. Avoid weekends in mid-summer; it is crowded with Swiss families.

A LAKE THAT REFLECTS LIKE GLASS

Tseuzier Lake

A reservoir behind Crans-Montana. When the wind drops, the water goes still enough to mirror the peaks on the far shore.

Drive up the Rawyl road from Ayent. The lake sits at the top. Park at the dam, then walk the path along the north shore. The best reflections are at sunrise in late October when the larches on the surrounding slopes turn gold.

Wide lens for the whole vista, or a tele to isolate the reflected ridge. Mornings are almost always calmer than afternoons here; if the wind is up, walk to the far end where the water is more sheltered.

A HIDDEN LAKE IN A HANGING VALLEY

Salanfe Lake

A reservoir that sits in a bowl between the Dents du Midi. Harder to reach than it looks on the map. That is the point.

Accessible via Van d'en Haut above Salvan. Park where the road ends and hike about 2 hours up. Alternative: approach from Mex in the Rhône valley, longer but quieter. Either way, the climb opens out suddenly to a huge still lake ringed by walls of rock.

Stay overnight at the Cabane de Salanfe if you can book it. Dawn on the lake is among the best in the canton. A drone works well up here but the winds can pick up fast, so keep your eye on the treeline and do not fly far.

FROZEN PINES, A TWENTY-MINUTE WINDOW

La Tzoumaz

A high-alpine forest above Verbier where, on cold spring mornings, the line between frozen and green pines cuts through the slope.

Go in spring, early, before the sun gets to the trees. The frozen pines melt fast. Sometimes in twenty minutes. So you need to be set up before first light. The contrast between the icy forest and the slopes already warmed through is what you came for.

Park near La Tzoumaz and walk up toward the treeline. Any direction works; the effect is everywhere if the conditions cooperated overnight. A telephoto compresses the transition line into a clean graphic.

AccessDrive + short walk

SUMMITS

Pointe du Tsaté

A long hike to a quiet summit overlooking the Moiry Glacier. The kind of view you earn.

The summit looks straight onto the Moiry Glacier from the ridge above the cabane. Long approach, real elevation gain, but the perspective on the glacier is one you cannot get from the hut viewpoint.

Plan a full day or split it with a night at Cabane des Becs de Bosson. Solid hiking shoes, water, and check the weather. The ridge is exposed.
