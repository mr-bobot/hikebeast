What's new

Quick log of what just shipped and what we're working on right now.

Working on now

  • Adding hikes

    Over 100 hikes coming, directly integrated with SwissTopo so you can see the route on the map.

  • Adding public transport

    Public-transport directions for each spot so you know the best train + bus combo to get there.

Recently shipped

  • Google Earth support May 24

    Every spot in a single .kml file you can open in Google Earth · spin the globe, swoop into a valley, get a feel for the terrain before you go.

  • Affiliate program May 24

    An affiliate system where selected people can earn a commission. Email leon@hikebeast.ch if you want to be considered.

  • Wildcamping section redone May 17

    16 curated spots split by camping style (tent area, tent + bivouac, bivouac only) with a filter chip at the top. Fronalpstock, Moiry Glacier, Gastlosen, and others added.

  • Explore + Home remember where you were May 15

    For You row and the photo wall now stay put when you click into a spot and come back. Reshuffles after an hour, or hit the new Shuffle button on Explore.

  • Dark mode May 13

    Optional dark theme across the whole webapp. Toggle in the rail on desktop, or in the Settings section of the mobile menu.

  • Faster photos, no flicker May 13

    Image grid renders at its final size on first paint instead of reflowing once photos decode. Killed the "image reloads itself" flash on Home and Explore.

  • How to get there May 13

    Every spot now has a proper access paragraph: where to park, which cable car, how long the walk is. Filled in for all 124 spots.

  • Wildcamping research May 11

    Used AI to deep-research every spot's legal camping status (BLN, federal reserves, cantonal rules) and grounded each verdict in actual federal and cantonal sources, not guesswork.

  • Submit your own photos May 10

    Any spot page has a button to upload your own shot. We review submissions and add the best ones to the gallery, with photo credit.

  • Photo infos

    Tap any photo on a spot page and you see who took it and where, with a link to the photographer's profile when we have it.

  • Been there

    Mark the spots you've actually visited. Builds a personal travel log you can flip through anytime, separate from your liked list.

  • Likes

    Heart any spot to save it for later. Your liked list lives in the rail and syncs across devices when you're signed in.

  • Swipe (Tinder style)

    One spot at a time. Swipe right to like, left to skip. Fastest way to triage 140+ spots and build your shortlist for the season.

  • Random

    Jump to a random spot you haven't decided on yet. Good for when you want a surprise instead of scrolling.

  • Explore (Pinterest style)

    All the photos on one wall, masonry layout. Filter by region or type (lake, glacier, ridge, …), search by name, save what catches your eye.

  • Map

    Every spot pinned on a Swiss map. Region-coloured markers, popups with the photo and name, jump straight into any spot.
