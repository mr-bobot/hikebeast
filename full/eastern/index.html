EASY CABLE CAR, BIG RIDGE

Schäfler

A 2-hour walk from Ebenalp to the Schäfler ridge in Appenzell. One of the best classic hikes in Eastern Switzerland.

Take the cable car to Ebenalp, then hike up to Schäfler. Moderate pace, obvious trail. There's a chalet at the top where you can sleep. Book ahead.

Sunset is why you come here. The Alpstein ridges light up and the mist in the valleys to the north goes pink. If you stay the night, sunrise is equally strong.

AccessCable car + 2 hr hike

SWISS POSTCARD, ACTUAL VERSION

Seealpsee

A glassy alpine lake below Säntis. You can rent a little boat for five francs and row out into the reflection.

On the way down from Schäfler, stop at Äscher and then keep going to Seealpsee. The boat rental is cheap and being on the water gives you a completely different angle than the shoreline shots.

Just before sunset is the moment. The water calms, the peaks around go warm, and the day-trippers have started heading down.

THE CHALET IN THE CLIFF

Äscher

The famous Swiss chalet built into a cliff face. Busy for a reason. The scene is as good as the postcards claim.

On the way down from Schäfler to Seealpsee, follow the yellow 'Äscher' signs. It sits wedged under a limestone overhang, with a terrace that looks out over the valley.

Anytime light is fine. Mid-afternoon is when the sun hits the cliff; overcast means the wood texture reads better. Weekdays are way quieter than weekends.

A NOTCH IN THE RIDGE

Saxer Lücke

A dramatic cleft between two peaks in the Churfirsten. Short hike, huge payoff.

Take the cable car from Frümsen up to Gasthaus Staubern, then hike to Saxer Lücke. It's an easy walk along the ridge. Well-maintained trail, family-friendly.

Sunset is the hit. The light comes through the notch and the whole Churfirsten lights up. Bring a tripod; blue hour hangs around for a while up there.

RESERVOIR, TUNNEL, THEN MOUNTAINS

Limmernsee

A turquoise reservoir above Linthal, reached via a funicular and a long tunnel. Strange approach, stunning arrival.

Take the Luftseilbahn Tierfehd-Kalktrittli. Double-check the schedule, it usually only runs mid-June to end of September. From the top, you cross a tunnel and climb toward the dam.

Sunrise and sunset both work; overcast goes moody. The water colour is most vivid in late summer. Bring layers. Even in August it can be cold up there.

SLEEP ABOVE THE TURQUOISE

Muttseehütte

A mountain hut just above Limmernsee. Overnight here and you get both sunset and sunrise from the ridge.

Same approach as Limmernsee. Funicular, tunnel, then climb. Book the hut in advance (muttseehuette.ch, or call). It fills up fast in peak season.

Dinner at the hut, drinks on the terrace while the light goes. Cloudy conditions are actually stronger than blue skies here. Moodier water colour, better mountain mood.

SOFT LIGHT, LONG EXPOSURES

Berschnerfall

A tall waterfall near Walenstadt. Cloudy days flatter it; bring a tripod and a blurred foreground.

Compose with something in the foreground. Moss, ferns, a wet branch. For depth. The classic mistake is shooting the falls head-on with no lead-in element.

Watch your highlights. The white of the water clips fast even on overcast days. Underexpose slightly, recover in post.

A HIGH PASS WITH ROOM TO CAMP

Klausenpass

The pass road itself, plus a short hike to 46.866729, 8.859118 to find a spot close to where we've camped.

Park at the Klausenpass and hike toward the GPS point. The terrain opens up and there's a flat area that works as a bivy spot if you're set up for it.

Sunrise is the goal. The peaks around the pass catch first light clean. Stay until the valley warms up, then descend.

A SHORT WALK, LOTS OF WATER IN SPRING

Bärglistüber

An easy-access waterfall in Glarus canton. Visit in May or June for maximum flow.

Short walk from the road to the falls. Peak water is in May-June as the snow above melts; later in summer it's significantly reduced.

Cloudy days or shade hours work best. Direct sun makes the whites impossible to handle. Bring a tripod for long exposures if that's your thing.

LIMESTONE THAT LOOKS ITALIAN

The Swiss Dolomites

A stretch of peaks around Klausenpass that honestly reminds me of Italy. Worth a stop even without a plan.

GPS: 46.905367, 8.940339. Park nearby, walk up a ridge, and you'll see why I started calling these the Swiss Dolomites. Jagged, bone-grey limestone, zero crowd.

Sunrise or moody overcast both deliver. Blue-sky blue-hour days are actually the weakest here. The rock lacks contrast against a clean sky.

FLOATING ICE IN SUMMER

Ice Lake (Griesslisee)

A small high lake that holds ice in its water into the middle of summer. Hike-in, moderate effort, surreal result.

Start from Klausenpass. Not too hard, not too easy. A moderate grade, plan a half-day round trip.

Go in the middle of summer. Too early and there's still snow around the lake; too late and the ice has gone. The sweet spot is usually mid-July to early August.

A CROSS ON THE HILL, THE BEST VIEW

Gigerwaldsee

A turquoise reservoir near Vättis. From the village, look for the cross on the hillside. There's a path up to it.

The path behind the little village takes you up to the cross you can see from below. From up there, you have the cleanest angle on the lake, colour and curve together.

Anytime light works. Midday actually makes the water turquoise pop hardest; cloudy softens it but reads more moody. Pick your poison.

PHOTOGENIC. BUT A BIT SOULLESS

Caumasee

Famous, teal-coloured, and honestly disappointing in person. I'll tell you what I actually think of it.

Caumasee looks spectacular on camera. In person, they've turned it into an outdoor swimming-pool operation, and there isn't much nature left around the shore. Not a favourite of mine.

If you're there anyway. Shoot at sunrise before the complex opens, or walk to Crestasee nearby for something more honest (see the next page).

A DEEP CANYON IN THE EAST

Swiss Grand Canyon

The Ruinaulta. A white limestone canyon carved by the Rhine. From the viewpoint, it really does look like the States.

From Caumasee, follow the hiking signs to the Ruinaulta viewpoint. It's a short walk to the rim; the drop is big enough to be careful near the edge.

Sunrise is the light. The east face of the canyon catches first light and the mist often sits low in the bottom of the gorge. Twenty minutes later it's gone.

QUIETER THAN CAUMASEE, WAY BETTER

Crestasee

A small pine-fringed lake nearby. No fences, no crowds, just pines and glass-still water on a calm day.

When there's no wind, the reflections hit. The pines around the shore double in the water and the whole scene looks painted.

Anytime light works. Early morning is best for wind (there usually isn't any); late afternoon gives you warmer tones. Avoid weekend afternoons in summer. It's the local swim spot.

SUP, CAMPER VAN, LAKE

Klöntalersee

A long, narrow mountain lake in Glarus canton. Beautiful to paddle and one of the best van-life spots in the region.

Bring a stand-up paddle board, or rent one locally. The lake is wind-protected most of the time and long enough to make a proper paddling day of it.

Sunset is the best light. Camper vans are tolerated at designated pull-offs. Be respectful, leave nothing. The cliffs on the north side catch gold light late.

CHAPELS

Stoss Kirche

A small wooden chapel on a green knoll with craggy Appenzell peaks behind. The shot composes itself.

Stoss is a saddle above Altstätten in the Appenzell ridge. You can drive to the parking at the pass and walk five minutes to the chapel.

Time it for afternoon when the clouds pile up behind the peaks. The church in the bottom third, a big sky on top.

The detail that makes it work is the yellow wood against the grey limestone. Don't oversaturate it. Let the contrast do the talking.

AccessDrive to Stoss, 5 min walk

ALPINE LAKES

Pizol

The famous five-lake walk, but the detail most people miss is the cairn field on the upper ridge at sunset.

Take the cable car from Wangs-Pizolbahn up to Furt and follow the 5-Seen-Wanderung. Plan five to six hours for the full loop.

The cairn field near the high point is the shot. Hundreds of stacked stones catching low light. You need to stay up past sunset or arrive before sunrise.

ALPINE LAKES

Jöriseen

A string of impossibly turquoise lakes in a grey cirque above Davos. Worth every metre of elevation.

From Flüelapass, take the path up past the Winterlücke to the Jörifluessee saddle. Four hours one way if you are moving.

The colour is glacial flour. It reads even stronger under grey skies, so do not write off a cloudy day.

A figure on the switchback trail adds scale. Do not crop them out, you need something to anchor the frame.

LAKES

Fälensee

Saxer Lücke gets the crowds. Drop below the ridge and you find this narrow lake wedged between walls, almost nobody around.

From Saxer Lücke, take the trail south-east and descend toward Bollenwees. Fälensee sits right at the base, a stretched ribbon of water.

The view that sells it is from the ridge above, looking straight down the lake. Telephoto, 85 to 135mm, to compress the walls.

Clouds rolling through the gap at the end of the lake are the bonus. Stick around if you see fog moving in.

WATERFALLS

Batöni Wasserfallarena

A whole arena of waterfalls raining down a cirque at the head of Weisstannental. Proper scale without the crowds.

Drive up Weisstannental to Schwendi, then hike in toward Batöni. Around two hours to reach the main amphitheatre.

Early summer is the peak, when the snowmelt is going. Go for the long lens and compress the valley walls and the falls together.

AN ICE RIVER IN ENGADIN

Morteratsch Glacier

A big, walkable glacier near Pontresina. And one of the clearest examples of retreat in the country.

Never walk onto the glacier without a guide. Crevasses are real and not always visible. From the train station, yellow signs lead to viewpoints along the moraine.

For the best shot, drone a top-down of the glacier tongue. Anytime light works. Overcast makes the texture read properly, which is actually the best option here.

AccessTrain + walk / guide
EffortEasy to moderate

HUT ABOVE THE GLACIER

Bovalhütte

A SAC hut perched above Morteratsch Glacier. Classic view, great base if you want to go higher.

From Morteratsch train station, follow the yellow 'Bovalhütte' signs. You'll climb above and to the right of the glacier tongue. Solid path, moderate effort.

Anytime light works up there. Stay for a night and you get both sunrise and sunset; the glacier colour shifts from silver to pink as the day turns.
