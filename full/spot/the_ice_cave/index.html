DEEP WINTER ONLY, AND ONLY IF IT'S COLD

The Ice Cave

A natural tunnel under Zinal Glacier. One of the most photogenic spots in Valais. And one of the easiest to get hurt in if you go at the wrong time.

Start near coords 46.127840, 7.629728 and hike toward 46.071559, 7.632709. This is a deep-winter spot. Never go after warm days, and do not go alone. Ice caves collapse when temperatures rise; people die from that every year in the Alps. Check avalanche reports, tell someone your plan.

Inside, light bounces blue off the ice. A wide lens gets the whole tunnel arc; a mid-telephoto picks out the frozen detail. Bring crampons or microspikes, headlamp, and a tripod. It is darker than it looks.
