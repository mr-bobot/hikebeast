A GLACIER YOU CAN STILL REACH ON FOOT

Ferpècle Glacier

Tucked at the head of the Val d'Hérens, quieter than its neighbours and wild in a way Zermatt no longer is.

Drive to the hamlet of Ferpècle at the end of the road, park, and walk in toward the glacier front. About 2 hours of mostly flat to gently rising terrain. You will share the valley with a handful of day hikers and some very serious-looking goats.

Bring a drone if you have one. The glacier and the surrounding Dent Blanche massif are best read from a bit of altitude. On the ground, look for the ice caves that form in the terminal face in late winter.
