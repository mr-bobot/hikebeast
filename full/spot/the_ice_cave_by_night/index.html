THE SAME PLACE, 12 HOURS LATER

The Ice Cave by Night

Headlamp, tripod, and 30 seconds of patience. The tunnel becomes something else entirely.

Same location, same winter-only rules. But shot after dark with the light source inside the cave. Set the camera to f/2.8, ISO around 1000, 20 to 30 second exposure. Have a friend stand still at the far end holding a warm-temperature headlamp; the ice walls will glow.

Dress warmer than you think. You will be standing in a wind tunnel made of ice. Kit that survives: weather-sealed body, two batteries kept inside your jacket, hand warmers taped to the lens to stop fogging when you exit.
