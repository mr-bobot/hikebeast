A SEA OF CLOUDS OVER THE PRE-ALPS

Gastlosen

A jagged limestone ridge on the Fribourg-Bern border. Best when an inversion rolls in and the peaks float above the fog.

Drive up toward Le Chalet du Soldat and hike a short while to the saddle that looks over the whole range. Sunrise is the best shot. Inversions happen more often in the early morning than in the evening.

Drone works well up here. If the sky is clear, handheld is fine too. Bring a telephoto to compress the ridge line into that postcard silhouette.

DINNER WITH A VIEW

Ritzli-Alp

A mountain restaurant on the way back from Gastlosen, with a direct line of sight on the ridge at blue hour.

Great way to end a long hiking day. Eat well, let the light go, and watch the Gastlosen turn pink behind the plate. It sits right on the descent, so no detour needed.

Stick around after sunset. Blue hour here is often better than the sunset itself. The sky goes deep indigo and the restaurant warm lamps add a second glow in the foreground.

GO WITH A GUIDE THE FIRST TIME

Latine Canyon

A narrow slot canyon near Château-d'Œx. Beautiful water, real risk. This one needs a local before you come back solo.

Also called La Tine. There's a dam in the upper valley, which means flash flood risk if you don't know the signals. Don't walk in blind. Book a trip with Gruyère Escapade (gruyere-escapade.ch/canyoning.html), learn the terrain, then come back on your own.

Once you know where to stand, the canyon is anytime. Shade means soft light almost all day. Bring a tripod and protect your camera from spray.

A DISTANT PEAK, A LONG LENS

Dent de Broc

Seen from the Moléson road, the Dent lines up just above a meadow. A compressed, minimalist frame if you have the reach.

On the way up to Moléson from the bottom parking, stop at 46.554424, 7.032846. The peak pops above the foreground meadow perfectly from here.

You'll want a 100 to 400mm to isolate the mountain properly. A 50mm makes it look tiny. Sunrise and sunset both work. Pick the direction of the wind so you don't lose the meadow to haze.

THE CLASSIC FRIBOURG LAKE

Schwarzsee

A small alpine lake ringed by limestone peaks. Looks like a calendar cover when the sun catches it right.

Arrive at sunrise, before the road fills up. The lake is quiet for maybe 45 minutes after first light. After that, the day trippers start showing up.

Don't shoot when the sun is high. You'll lose all contrast. Keep the sun low in your frame, or wait for cloud cover to soften the scene.

ICE HOCKEY, MOUNTAINS, GO

Winter Schwarzsee

Same lake, different season. When the ice is thick enough, you can skate. And play hockey under the peaks.

Call the tourism office before you drive up. The ice needs to be checked every year; they'll tell you if it's safe. Some winters it's perfect, some it never fully freezes.

Great with friends. Bring skates, a couple of sticks, a puck. There's no more Swiss scene than hockey on a natural rink with big walls of limestone around you.

A SHARP TRIANGLE ABOVE LES PACCOTS

Dent de Lys

A near-perfect pyramid peak you can hike up from Les Paccots. The ridge is steeper than it looks from the valley.

Start in Les Paccots, follow the yellow signs. The summit section gets exposed. Be careful in wet or windy weather. People have gotten hurt on this one, so take it seriously.

From the top: Léman south, the Jura west, the Alps everywhere else. Sunrise and sunset both deliver, but sunrise gives you the lake with mist underneath.

HIGH RIDGES NEAR THE CABLE CAR

Moléson

Take the cable car up and you're a few minutes from ridge walks that feel far more remote than they actually are.

Head out on the ridgeline from the top station. Five minutes and you've left the crowd at the café behind. Watch your footing. Drops on both sides in places.

Cloudy days actually look better here than blue skies. The light goes moody and the foreground rock reads properly. Sunrise and sunset also work when conditions cooperate.

RIDGE WALK FROM MOLÉSON

Teysachaux

Another Fribourg pyramid. Link it to Moléson along the ridge for a full-day traverse, or park near the base and hike up.

Two options: the long way, traversing the ridges from Moléson, which is a proper day out. Or parking at 46.534395, 6.975091 and hiking straight up, which cuts it down to a couple of hours.

Same rules as Dent de Lys: exposed in places, not a casual walk. Worth the effort for the 360° at the top.
